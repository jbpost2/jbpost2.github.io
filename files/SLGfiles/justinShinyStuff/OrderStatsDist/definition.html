Theory behind the distribution of Order Statistics

Order Statistic - Let \(Y_1,Y_2,...,Y_n\) be independent and identically distributioned with cdf \(F_Y(y)\) and pdf \(f_Y(y)\). Then $$Y_{(1)}=min{Y_1,Y_2,...,Y_n}$$ $$Y_{(2)}=second~smallest~Y$$ $$\vdots$$ $$Y_{(n)}=max{Y_1,Y_2,...,Y_n}$$ are called order statistics.

Each order statistics will have a distribution. We will assume that our Random Variables are continuous so we will not have to worry about ties.
